Why would you extract audio from a video?

There are many great reasons to pull audio out of a video file:

  • Save a song or soundtrack from a music video or movie clip

What video formats are supported?

FileFlip's audio extractor supports the most common video formats:

  • MP4 — the most common video format, used by phones, cameras, and video platforms
  • MOV — Apple's video format, used by iPhones and Mac cameras
  • AVI — older Windows video format, still widely used
  • MKV — popular for high-quality video downloads
  • WEBM — web-based video format used by browsers

What quality is the output MP3?

FileFlip extracts audio using variable bitrate encoding (VBR) at high quality settings — approximately 190kbps average. This produces excellent audio quality that's virtually indistinguishable from the source video's audio track for most content.

Keep in mind that the output MP3 quality is limited by the quality of the audio in the original video. If the video had low-quality audio, the extracted MP3 will reflect that — we can only work with what's in the original file.

Is extracting audio from videos legal?

This depends entirely on what you're extracting and what you plan to do with it. Extracting audio for personal use — like saving a video lecture for studying or keeping a backup of content you own — is generally fine. However, downloading copyrighted music from YouTube videos or distributing extracted audio commercially may violate copyright law. Always respect the rights of content creators.

Frequently asked questions

FileFlip works with video files you have saved on your device. To extract audio from YouTube, you would first need to download the video (check YouTube's terms of service and local laws before doing so), then use FileFlip to convert the video file to MP3.

What if my video has no audio?

If your video file doesn't contain an audio track, the conversion will fail with an error message. This can happen with screen recordings, animated GIFs saved as MP4, or videos where audio was removed.

Is there a file size limit?

FileFlip supports video files up to 50MB. For longer videos, consider trimming to just the audio you need before uploading, or use desktop software like VLC or Audacity for large file processing.
