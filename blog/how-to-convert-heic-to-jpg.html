What is HEIC and why does my iPhone use it?

HEIC stands for High Efficiency Image Container. Apple introduced it as the default photo format for iPhones starting with iOS 11 in 2017. The reason Apple switched to HEIC is simple — it produces images that are about 50% smaller in file size compared to JPG while maintaining the same or better visual quality.

This means you can store roughly twice as many photos on your iPhone compared to JPG. For Apple users staying within the Apple ecosystem (iPhone, iPad, Mac), HEIC works great. The problem arises when you try to use those photos outside of Apple's world.

Why can't I open HEIC files on Windows or Android?

HEIC is not a universally supported format. While Apple devices handle it natively, most Windows computers, Android phones, and web platforms don't support HEIC by default. This creates frustrating situations like:

  • Uploading an iPhone photo to a website and getting an "unsupported format" error
  • Sending photos to a friend on Android who can't open them
  • Trying to edit an iPhone photo in software that doesn't recognize HEIC
  • Submitting photos for a job application or school assignment that requires JPG
💡 Did you know?

Windows 10 and 11 can open HEIC files if you install the free "HEIF Image Extensions" from the Microsoft Store. However converting to JPG is still the most reliable solution for sharing with others.

How to stop your iPhone from shooting in HEIC

If you'd prefer your iPhone to always save photos as JPG instead of HEIC, you can change this in your camera settings:

  1. Open the Settings app on your iPhone
  2. Scroll down and tap Camera
  3. Tap Formats
  4. Select Most Compatible instead of "High Efficiency"

After this change your iPhone will save all new photos as JPG. Note that this uses more storage space since JPG files are larger than HEIC.

📤 Sharing tip

When you share photos from your iPhone using AirDrop to a Mac, they stay as HEIC. But when you share to a non-Apple device or via email, iOS automatically converts them to JPG. If you're sharing to Windows, use the share sheet and select "Mail" or "Messages" for automatic conversion.

What's the difference between HEIC and HEIF?

HEIF (High Efficiency Image Format) is the standard, and HEIC (High Efficiency Image Container) is Apple's implementation of it. They're essentially the same thing. FileFlip supports both .heic and .heif file extensions.
